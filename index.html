About

Hello! I am Yasuhiro "Yasu" Sekiyama.

From teaching across cultures to solving technical challenges, I thrive on turning complex problems into simple solutions.

Before becoming a software engineer, I spent time teaching over 300 students in South Korea on a Fulbright Scholarship. As a cultural ambassador, I stepped outside traditional curricula to bring American culture to life, igniting curiosity and a passion for learning in my students. This cross-cultural experience shaped my adaptability, empathy, and communication skills.

After the Fulbright, I wore many hats, including working as a marketing associate at Sunmerry Bakery, where I helped promote and grow the brand. I also pursued a post-baccalaureate program in medicine at University of Southern California, but ultimately, I found that my passion lay elsewhere.

My journey into software engineering was sparked by a desire to find more passion in my professional life. Friends saw how much I enjoyed analyzing player stats in fantasy basketball and encouraged me to pursue a career in data. This led me to discover software engineering, where the thrill of solving complex problems gave me a sense of accomplishment I'd been seeking.

I honed my skills at App Academy, where I developed full-stack applications like "Fantasy Basketball Notebook". This capstone project combined my love for sports, data, and software development, using technologies like React, Flask, and an NBA API to enhance the user experience. I am continuously driven by learning and innovation, and I thrive on working in collaborative environments where empathy and teamwork are valued.
